Disc texture generator. On load, this page reads ./disc_template.png (a 16×16 template) and classifies every pixel by hex-distance to pure red vs. pure yellow, records each pixel's luminance offset from that reference color, and records its alpha. Those three per-pixel arrays (group, luminance delta, alpha) are then replayed onto your chosen main / ring colors — group 0 pixels get the main color, group 1 pixels get the ring color, each re-lit by its stored luminance delta and masked by its stored alpha. Nothing about the disc's shading is hand-authored; it's all read out of the template.

Colors are pulled from the embedded cover art (or the filename-derived art if none is embedded): the page buckets vivid pixels by hue, picks the most common hue as the main color and the next most common hue at least 40° away as the ring color, and only falls back to near-black/near-white if the art is genuinely achromatic.

Compiling the Fabric mod is left to you on purpose — the zip includes a complete Gradle project; run ./gradlew build yourself. This keeps everything, including the mod build, entirely on your machine.

Geyser: a geyser/custom_items.json mapping file is included so Bedrock players connected through Geyser see the correct icon; drop it into your Geyser custom_mappings the way Geyser's docs describe, since Geyser needs its own item mapping regardless of your resource pack.
